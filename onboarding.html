3Task 3 · Why People Quit
acknowledge_list Edit

Acknowledge each obstacle

Lack of clear goals⌄
Vague start, no finish line. Without something concrete to aim at, drift wins.
Poor planning⌄
Underestimated time, money, or effort — the project hits an early wall and stalls.
Loss of motivation⌄
Excitement fades after the initial push. Daily check-ins are designed to counter exactly this.
Competing priorities⌄
Life, work, or family demands take over. Energy budget keeps it sustainable.
Resource constraints⌄
Funding, tools, or people unavailable — we'll inventory all of this in Step 3.
Skill gaps⌄
The project needs abilities you don't have yet. We'll surface these and find help.
Fear of failure⌄
Avoidance when results feel uncertain. Small wins reframe the whole arc.
Perfectionism⌄
Endless tweaking, never calling it done. "Good enough" ships.
Weak accountability⌄
No deadlines or external pressure. We'll set one in Task 4.
Scope creep⌄
Project keeps expanding beyond control. We'll define a clear scope and protect it.

5Task 5 · How important is this?

How important is this project to you?

1 — Not important
Finishing is optional; the value is mostly in exploring or testing.
2 — Mildly important
Completion is nice-to-have but the project still delivers learning or insight without it.
3 — Moderately important
Finishing matters, but partial progress still yields meaningful benefit.
4 — Very important
The project's value is largely tied to reaching the end-state.
5 — Critical
Failure to complete defeats the entire purpose of the project.
