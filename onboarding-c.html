Task 2 - Requirements Gathering⌄

Great. Now I want to know what we're actually working with — the conditions, the constraints.

I'll ask the 10 most important questions for a project like this. Multiple-choice, quick.

⌃PurposeVoice

What's the main purpose of this garden? Food, beauty, pollinators, relaxation — this defines everything else.

⌃SunlightVoice

How much sun does the area actually get — and for how many hours a day? Sunlight determines 90% of what can live there. Don't guess — measure.

⌃SoilVoice

What's the soil type and condition? Clay, loam, sand — plus drainage, pH, and nutrients. Without this, everything's guesswork.

⌃WateringVoice

What's your watering situation? Hose, irrigation, rainfall, buckets — affects plant choice and layout more than design.

⌃MaintenanceVoice

How much maintenance are you willing to do, honestly? A Saturday hobby or a lifestyle? This filters high-care vs. low-maintenance plants.

⌃ClimateVoice

What's your local climate zone or frost-date range? Tells planting windows, overwintering needs, and perennial survival odds.

⌃PestsVoice

Do you have pests or wildlife to account for? Deer, rabbits, raccoons, grubs — the sooner you plan defenses, the better.

⌃SpaceVoice

What's your available space and layout potential? Flat yard vs. slope, raised beds vs. ground, containers — shapes what's feasible.

⌃BudgetVoice

What's your budget range for setup and upkeep? Soil, tools, plants, mulch, irrigation — better to be real now than broke in July.

⌃DurationVoice

How long do you want this garden to last? Seasonal experiment, annual refresh, or multi-year perennial design — defines planning depth.
