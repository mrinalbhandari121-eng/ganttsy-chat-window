What to Expect

▴ Health · Transition forward a toddler diet

By now, many babies are eating a wide variety of solids alongside milk, with increasing participation in family meals. Appetite may fluctuate, especially with growth changes or teething. The focus shifts toward balanced nutrition, exposure, and self-feeding skills rather than intake perfection (American Academy of Pediatrics, World Health Organization).

By month 12, feeding shifts from pure nutrition delivery to skill-building and habit formation. Your child is learning how to eat — textures, flavors, timing, and social cues. Appetite will vary widely day-to-day due to growth, activity, and teething, which is normal. Milk still plays a role, but solids now supply broader nutrient exposure and autonomy. Eating alongside family becomes important, as imitation drives learning. The goal is not perfect intake — it's developing a positive, low-pressure relationship with food that supports long-term nutrition and behavior.

Pro Tip

Serve the same core foods repeatedly even if rejected. It can take 10–15 exposures before acceptance. Consistency beats variety overload.
